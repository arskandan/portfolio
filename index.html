Master Graduation Project – Soft Robotics

Eindhoven University of Technology | Dec. 2024 – Sep. 2025 | Eindhoven, Netherlands

Electronic skin (e-skin) is an artificial sensing layer that allows robots to perceive external contact in a way similar to human skin, which is essential for safe and adaptive human-robot interaction. However, no single sensing principle is sufficient on its own: pneumatic pressure sensors can measure the magnitude of applied forces but provide no information related to contact location, whereas capacitive proximity sensors can localize contact but cannot quantify force. To overcome these limitations, this project develops a sensor fusion framework that integrates a pneumatic pressure sensor with a capacitive proximity sensor in a large-area 3D-printed TPU e-skin.

The system was experimentally validated through controlled indenter tests and finger-touch trials across multiple regions of the skin surface. Results show that the fused approach achieves sub-centimeter contact localization (RMSE approximately 1.05 cm) and provides position-aware force estimation with up to 12% higher accuracy compared to a uniform effective elastic modulus model, especially near the skin boundaries where stiffness varies significantly. These improvements demonstrate the effectiveness of sensor fusion in capturing both the location and magnitude of contact, thereby advancing the design of large-area e-skins in robotics.
